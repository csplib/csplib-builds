The magic hexagon was found in 1895 after much trial and error by William Radcliffe [radcliffe].

In 1963, Charles Trigg [trigg] proved that it is the only magic hexagon of any size (other than the trivial diameter 1) using a constraint on the sum of any diagonal.

See the related magic square and sequence problem entry.
